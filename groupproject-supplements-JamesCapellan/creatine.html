Creatine-Monohydrate
( Creatine )

What is Creatine?

Creatine is a naturally occurring compound found in skeletal muscle that is synthesized in the body from amino acids and can be obtained from red meat and seafood. In the body, it helps produce adenosine triphosphate (ATP), which provides energy for muscles. Creatine is a popular workout supplement marketed to increase athletic performance, especially for weight training.
Essentially, it is a a dietary supplement that increases muscle performance in short-duration, high-intensity resistance exercises.

'Creatine is among the most well-researched and effective supplements.' - Kamel Patel, Examine.com
There are common misconceptions of danger from taking creatine. (Loss of hair, testicular shrinkage, etc...)
However, Most of the supposed dangers of creatine are unfounded. Due to the bad reputation of performance-enhancing drugs, it has been wrongfully equated with the worst examples.

How do you take Creatine?

Creatine is most commonly found in powder form, micronized creatine monohydrate, which dissolves in water more easily and can be more practical. Often sold in tubs or bags.

  • It is recommended that 5g (one scoop) of Creatine is consumed daily, whether that be dissolved in water or a protein shake.
  • Individuals may sometimes choose to enter a 'loading phase' of creatine, which involves taking higher quantities weeks before in order to saturate their body cells with creatine.
  • Sensations of stomach cramps or discomfort may affect new users, but dissappear after extended use.
