New guys on the road: Attacus Cycling
Up-and-coming British entry into the cycling apparel scene.
James Capellan - 3/2/2022, 10:00 AM
Caption
Original
In 2015, London native, Jimmy Nichols took his ideas to pen and paper, rendering the first sketch-ups of Attacus apparel.
Enamoured with riding his bicycle from a young age, the Attacus co-founder was dissatisfied with the market price of modern cycling kit: commonly spending over £1000 seasonally.
“I couldn't justify spending that much money even though I knew it was good stuff,” he says. “I wanted to find something that was suitable and I couldn’t, so I thought: well I’ll just do it myself.”
Over the next 3 years, he paired up with his girlfriend Emily Chids to chip away at tasks that would ultimately lead to the release of their first collection. Prioritising good design and reasonable prices, this process understandably took time. During this period, Jimmy who was currently working in finance, was surprised to discover the true quantity of backing he required. From creating samples, to contacting suitable manufacturers, the couple worked overtime hours in their daily jobs to create a product that satisfied their standards.
By 2017, the duo realised that to give Attacus a chance at growing, they would have to invest more time into the company. Jimmy who was working in finance, left his job to work full-time on the front end. Emily gave up her field in journalism to put her skills at play in the back end.
Entering an already established market of heavy hitters laced with heritage in the sport, eg: Rapha, Assos, Castelli; Attacus had to find it’s own image to stay in the peloton. Following recent trends, Attacus jerseys feature minimalistic designs through a select colour palette based on the season. Their kit is made recognisable by their ‘chest stripes’ and the brand’s logo, inspired by the Attacus atlas moth, a symbol of their values: to go out and adventure.
Since its establishment, Attacus has spread it’s wings by sponsoring video creator and cycling media influencer, ‘Francis Cade’, as well as the ‘InternationalElles’ - a group of 10 women who cycled the 3,460km Tour De France. The brand continues to grow, and the couple are in the process of moving to a new headquarters.
Visit their website: https://attacus.cc/
